Single Qubit gates

Hadamard
The Hadamard gate rotates the qubit along the xy-axis. This gate brings the qubit into a superposition of 0 and 1.

Pauli gates
The Pauli gates rotate the qubit along the corresponding axes (X, Y, and Z).

S, T and π/6 phase-shift gates
These gates change the phase of the qubits (along the Z axis). The S gate is a π/2 phase-shift and the T is a π/4 phase-shift. In order to make use of them, the qubit has to be in a superposition of 0 and 1. In a pure 0 or 1 state, phase-shift has no effect.

<Smatrix>

<Tmatrix>

<P6matrix>

SQRT_NOT
Similarly to the Hadamard gate, the square root of the NOT gate brings the qubit into a superposition of 0 and 1 states, however, with a phase shift of -π/2.

<Qmatrix>

Identity
The identity matrix keeps the qubit in its current state (i.e does nothing).

<Imatrix>


Multi Qubit gates
Multi qubit gates act on a pair or more qubits, and usually they have one or more control and target qubits. A control qubit influences how the gate will act on the target qubit. Sometimes, however, both qubits are equal, eg. in the SWAP gate.

CNOT
It is perhaps the most important gate in working with entanglement on quantum computers. To put things simple, it basically rotates the target qubit around its x-axis if the control qubit is measured 1 right after the application of the gate. It is a controlled Pauli-gate.

<CNOTmatrix>

Controlled Y
Similarly to CNOT, it performs a rotation if the control qubit is measured 1, however, around the y-axis. This gate is also a controlled Pauli gate.

<CYmatrix>

Controlled Z
As the above two gates, this gate performs a rotation along the Z-axis if the control qubit is measured 1. Besides being a controlled Pauli gate, this is also a controlled phase-shift gate with an angle π.

<CZmatrix>

Controlled S
Controlled S gate performs a relative phase shift of π/2 between the two qubits if the control qubit is 1.

<CSmatrix>

Controlled T
Controlled T gate performs a relative phase shift of π/4 between the two qubits if the control qubit is 1.

<CTmatrix>

Controlled Hadamard
The controlled version of the Hadamard gate.

<CHmatrix>

SWAP
The swap gate swaps the properties of 2 qubits.

<SWAPmatrix>

Toffoli
The most common 3 qubit gate performs a NOT operation on the target qubit if both control qubits are measured 1.

<CCXmatrix>

Fredkin
